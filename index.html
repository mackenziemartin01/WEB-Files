Giant pandas are one of the rarest mammales in the world. Being a previously endangered species, the animal is very protected by conservation efforts across the globe. Their fluffy black and white coat combined with a cute face grasps peoples hearts.

Where do they live?

Pandas are native to the mountains of central China. While they can be seen in select zoos around the globe, this is not wihtout strict regulation. Only 24 pandas live in captivity outside of China. They can be seen in four U.S. zoos in San Diego, Atltana, D.C. and Memphis. Habitat destruction and poaching are the biggest threats to the mammals existence. Conservationalists efforts to keep pandas under a close watch have helepd save the species.

Are they still endangered?

Due to some of the strict conservation efforts mentioned above, the giant panda is no longer endangered. The animal is protected by legislation such as the Convention on International Trade in Endangered Speciies of Wild Fauna and Flora. This is a treaty signed by 175 nations banning trade of endangered animals and plants. Zoo keepers amongst us all hope to see pandas live on.
